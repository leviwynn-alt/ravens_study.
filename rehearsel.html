The Rehearsel

Levi R Wynn

My refelction blinked a moment late- not enough to call it wrong, but just enough to fracture sense and prove i did not quite belong. It smiled then, too wide, too sure, with teeth that did not fit my name, Like something wearing me too tightly still unfamiliar with the frame. It's hands rose slowly to the glass, but not in time with mine at all they pressed with quiet certnity, as though it meant to make me fall. I tried to move, to test the space, to prove i was still the first, but every action felt delayed, Like i was echoing in reverse and then it spoke- without my leave- it's voice a mirror, cold and true: "you've never been alone in here. I've been rehearsing being you."
