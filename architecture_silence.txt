        <h2>The Architecture of Slince </h2>
        <p class="subtitle">Levi R Wynn</p>
		<p>
          I pried my jaw apart to scream,
		  and felt it tearing at the seams,
		  yet found it bound with thinking threads
		  that were not mine- yet lived in me,
		  coiled deep beneath what i could name,
		  each one a memory without origin,
		  each one aware i'd take the blame.
		  They stitched me shut from the inside out
		  through nerve and pulse and tremoring breath,
		  until each thought i tried to voice
		  collapsed before it reached it's death.
		  I fought- I tore- I begged release,
		  but every motion made them bind,
		  as though the act of wanting out
		  was what secured them to my mind
		  and something watched benath It all-
		  not cruel, not kind, but patient, still-
		  as if it knew i'd break myself
		  Long before I broke its will.
        </p>
