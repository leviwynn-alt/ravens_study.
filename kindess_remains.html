The Kindness That Remains

Levi R Wynn

The Dark has learned to imitate the sacred outlines of my trust- it moves with borrowed tenderness, yet breathes a faint iron rust. It shapes itself in voices known, it echos i have held most dear, until the line between the two dissolves into perfect fear. It does not rush, nor does it force- it waits as patient things will do. until i lean just close enough to hear what is no longer true. It calls my name as something wakes- not violently, but slow and sure, Like something ancient in the blood that suffering alone makes pure. And in that call, I feel it shift not just around me, but within, as though the dark i've learned to fear has always known me as kin
