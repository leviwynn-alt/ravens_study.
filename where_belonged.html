Where I Once Belonged

Levi R Wynn

You think you'd know- the instant comes, a break, a tear, a violent swift- some signal marking where you end, and something else begins to lift. But nothing break's. Nothing declares. No warning cut's the moment through. It happens slowly- so precise you never notice they do. A space is taken- not destored- just emptied, peice by carful peice, until the shape you where remains, but offers you no lease, you linger there, a perfect fit for something you cant seem to be, a hollow molded in your name, still shaped, but no longer there.
